Russia Announces Invasion of Ukraine


Ukrainian President's Speech in response to the invasion
On February 24th, Russian President Vladimir Putin announced his invasion of Ukraine via television, calling it a "Special Military Operation". Russia's alleged objectives for the invasion include the integration of the Donetsk People's Republic and Lughansk People's Republic, two breakaway puppet regions that have financially depended on Russia since their creation in 2014. The other stated objectives are "denazification" and "demilitarization" of the Ukrainian government, which, according to the Kremlin, has persecuted ethnic Russians. In addition, the Kremlin challenged Ukraine's right to exist.
Minutes after the announcement, Ukrainian territory was hit by several missiles and airstrikes, followed by a Russian Ground invasion from Belarus, Crimea, and Russia.

Other events on February 24th:

  • Ukraine begins mobilization, barring all males between 18 and 60 years old from leaving the country.
  • Martial Law is enacted in Ukraine, and Russo-Ukrainian relations are severed.
  • Russian airborne units briefly capture Hostomel Airport, located north of Kyiv. The airport is recaptured by Ukrainian forces by the end of the day. Eventually, the Russians would recapture the airport again before leaving permenantly by March 28th. According to some sources, local armed civillians also took part in the fighting. The world's largest airplane was also destroyed amid the fighting.
  • Russian forces capture the North Crimean Canal. At the canal, the Russian forces destroy a dam that prevented Crimea from accessing freshwater from the mainland.
  • Russia captures snake island, following an artillery barrage from the Moskva and the Vasily Bykov. Prior to the barrage, the Russian vessels ordered the thirteen Ukrainian soldiers to surrender the island. One of the Ukrainian soldiers responded by saying "Russian warship, go fuck yourself." The soldiers of the island later recieved Ukraine's highest award.

Russian Forces Reach Within 9 Kilometers of Kyiv


Ukrainian Territorial Defence Force soldiers in Kyiv on February 25th

The Ukrainian Defence Ministry reported on February 25th that Russian Forces were nine kilometers away from the Verkhovna Rada building, where the Ukrainian parliment meets. On the same day, Russian saboteur groups, dressed as Ukrainian soldiers, were also found and neutralized within Kyiv, while heavy fighting occured in the city of Vasilkiv, 35 kilometers south of Kyiv. In response, 18,000 assault rifles were distributed to volunteers who wished to join the Territorial Defense Forces, which are the reserves of the Ukrainian Ground Forces. Ukrainian Civillians also began to prepare molotov cocktails. Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky later posted a video of himself in Kyiv with Prime Minister Denys Shmyhal and Presidential Advisor Mykhailo Podoliak, in response to rumors that the president had fled the country.

Other Events on February 25th:

  • Russian President Vladimir Putin issued a televised speech calling for the Ukrainian Military to overthrow their own government.
  • A UN Security Council to condemn Russia's invasion of Ukraine was blocked by Russia. The Russian Ambassador, Vasily Nebenzya, claimed that footage of Russian artillery strikes are false, and that the United States was in "no position to moralize" due to its own previous wars.
  • Following the capture of Chernobyl, Russian forces advanced into Ivankiv, and were engaged by Ukrainian Forces there. By March 2nd, the city was captured by Russian Forces. Ivankiv would later be retaken by Ukrainian forces on March 31st.
  • The city of Chernehiv became surrounded by Russian forces. The 1st Tank Brigade, along with some Territorial Defence Force units, defended the city from various Russian Brigades until March 31st. Afterwards, Ukrainian Forces recaptured M01 highway and ended the seige.
  • A Ukrainian Su-27 was shot down in a friendly-fire incident over Kyiv.
  • Pentagon reports that 30% of Russia's invasion force has been committed to Ukraine.
  • Hostomel airport is recaptured by Russian forces, who would hold the airport until March 28th.
  • A Russian Il-76 transport plane carrying Russian soldiers bound for Hostomel was shot down by the Ukrainian air force.

Photo of General Sukhovetsky by Getty Images

Image By Mvs.gov.ua, CC BY 4.0, https://commons.wikimedia.org/w/index.php?curid=115915000

Power Loss In Chernobyl


Image of pro-Ukrainian protesters
